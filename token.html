Why do we reset our bot's tokens?

In this article, we will tell you why we reset our bots token every so often and how important it is to do so

"It's just like a common term you will have most likely heard "always change your password". As the more frequently you do this, you reduce the chances of someone cracking your password."


So, when you invite a bot to a server you give it permissions. Right? Well, this means that you are allowing the bot to perform certain actions in your server at any time. This means that if you give it a permission, such as "Kick Members", then the bot could possibly kick all of the memebrs of your server (that were lower than the bot) if the bot was hijacked.


Now, by this; we don't mean someone hacking into our host and  controlling it this way, we mean someone fetching our bot's token. A bot token is what allows our host that runs the bot to connect to the discord API in order to perform actions. If this token was gotten by a person with mallicious intent, it could result in the person connecting to the discord API as the bot and being able to control the bot into doing whatever they wish.


Though, while this seems all so bad, the chances of this actually happening is very unlikely, in fact, acording to howsecureismypassword.net, most common bot tokens would take about 713 untrigintillion years to crack. But, if our bot's token had been somehow leaked on a crimnal source, someone could fetch our bot's token and possibly use it to do something negative, most likely effecting your server.


This is where our occasional token reset comes in handy. It's just like a common term you will have most likely heard "always change your password". As the more frequently you do this, you reduce the chances of someone cracking your password. In the discord API's system, this is very much the same and this is why we have begun to introduce this infrequent bot token change which will take our bots offline for a few minutes, effecting usability and uptime, but will be vital in protecting you and every other server using our services. So we believe that this is a good trade-off
